## ‚ùì **WHY NO SIGNIFICANT RESULTS?**

### 1. **Small Sample Size** (n=15)
- With 15 participants, statistical power is **very low**
- Need r > ~0.51 to reach p < 0.05
- Many moderate correlations (r = 0.3-0.4) likely real but underpowered

### 2. **Personality Traits Are Self-Reported**
- May not reflect actual behavior
- Social desirability bias
- Self-perception ‚â† reality

### 3. **Summary Task Is Different from Conversation**
- Conversation traits (listening, expressing) ‚â† writing traits
- Summary writing is deliberate, not spontaneous
- People may engage different skills

### 4. **High Factual Accuracy Ceiling Effect**
- Mean factual accuracy = 96%
- Little variability ‚Üí hard to find correlations
- Most people got facts right regardless of personality

### 5. **Task May Override Personality**
- When given explicit instructions to summarize, people follow them
- Cognitive task demands may override personality tendencies
- Strong opinions don't leak into objective summary writing

## üìù **CONCLUSION**

**Main Finding**: Communication personality traits have **minimal to no relationship** with text summarization quality, except for a **moderate positive trend** between comfort with sensitive topics and factual accuracy (r = 0.475, p = 0.073).

**Implication**: People's self-reported communication styles do NOT predict how well they can objectively summarize opposing viewpoints. This suggests that **summary writing engages different cognitive processes** than conversation, and that people can **override personality tendencies when given explicit task instructions**.

**Limitation**: Small sample size (n=15) severely limits statistical power. Many moderate correlations (r = 0.3-0.4) may be real relationships that simply cannot be detected with this sample.
