2. Deterrence and the Death Penalty: A Study of the Effects of Capital Punishment on Homicide – Stump (Honors thesis, 2022)

Conclusion: even if some small deterrent effect exists, it’s weaker than other social factors, so the death penalty is not necessary for controlling homicide. 

3. Ethics of Death Penalty – Banerjee (India, 2024)

4. The Death Penalty Debate: a Look at the Main Arguments – Lantin (2017)

Stays relatively neutral, giving you clean formulations of both sides’ reasoning on each argument. 

5. Deterrence and the Death Penalty – The Death Penalty Project booklet
Argues there is no credible evidence that death sentences reduce homicide more than long prison terms.

6. Wrongful Convictions and the Death Penalty – The Death Penalty Project booklet
Documents at least 185 death-row exonerations in 29 U.S. states since 1973—about one wrongful conviction for every 8.3 executions.

Identifies recurring causes: false confessions (often under torture), bad lawyering, coerced pleas, mass trials, secrecy, mental disability, and weak forensic evidence. 

Bottom line: with an irreversible punishment, even a small error rate is catastrophic and structurally impossible to eliminate.

Compares 193 defendants in first-degree murder cases (death sought vs. not sought) in Oklahoma & Tulsa counties, 2004–2010.
