Facial Data (Emotional Signals)
- Basic Emotions (7): Angry, Disgust, Fear, Happy, Sad, Surprise, Neutral
- Emotion Intensity (per frame/second)
- Emotional Valence (positive vs negative affect)
- Arousal Level (activation/energy)
- Emotion Stability/Volatility (standard deviation over time)
- Dominant Emotion (most frequent)
- Emotional Transitions (changes between emotions)
- Facial Expression Intensity Heatmap (temporal patterns)
- Excitement vs Calmness Balance
- Positivity vs Negativity Ratio
- Emotional Quadrant Distribution (arousal-valence space)

Voice Data (Vocal Tone & Prosody)
- Voice Emotions (7): Angry, Disgust, Fear, Happy, Sad, Surprise, Neutral
  * All 7 emotions now displayed in comprehensive reports (matching facial system)
  * Emotions derived from acoustic features using rule-based classification
- Emotion Intensity (per audio segment)
- Voice Arousal (activation/energy in speech)
- Voice Valence (positive/negative tone)
- Voice Stress Level (pitch variation + harmonic ratio indicators)
- Dominant Voice Emotion (most frequent)
- Emotion Distribution (average probability across session)
- Pitch Mean (Hz) - average fundamental frequency
- Pitch Variation (Std Dev) - vocal stability indicator
- Pitch Range - difference between highest and lowest pitch
- Volume Mean (dB) - speaking loudness
- Volume Variation (Std Dev) - intensity consistency
- Volume Range - dynamic range of speech
- Spectral Centroid - brightness/timbre of voice (Hz)
- Spectral Bandwidth - frequency spread
- Spectral Rolloff - frequency distribution (85th percentile)
- Zero Crossing Rate - noisiness/voicing characteristic
- Speaking Rate (syllables or words per minute)
- Silence Ratio - proportion of non-speech segments
- Voice Tremor - vocal instability/shakiness
- Harmonic-to-Noise Ratio - voice quality indicator
- MFCC Coefficients (13 features) - voice quality signature/fingerprint
- Prosody Features (intonation patterns):
  * Energy variations
  * Intensity patterns
  * Jitter (pitch perturbation)
  * Shimmer (amplitude perturbation)
- Pause Duration and Frequency

Linguistic/Verbal Data (Text Analysis)
- Conversation Transcript (full text from ChatGPT records)
- Word Count and Speaking Time
- Politeness Markers (per minute): "please", "thank you", "I understand"
- Validation Words: "I see", "that makes sense", "interesting point"
- Confrontational Language: "no", "wrong", "disagree", "but"
- Question Frequency (genuine inquiry vs rhetorical)
- Perspective-Taking Language: "from your view", "you believe", "your position"
- Cognitive Processing Words: "think", "consider", "understand", "analyze"
- Emotional Words: positive vs negative sentiment
- First-Person vs Second-Person Pronouns (self-focus vs other-focus)
- Sentence Complexity and Length
- Interruption Patterns (if applicable)

Summary Accuracy Data (DV - Listening Index)
- Summary Completeness Score (% of main points captured)
- Summary Accuracy Score (correctness of restated stance)
- Mischaracterization Count (errors or distortions)
- Key Argument Recall (number of ChatGPT arguments remembered)
- Stance Identification (correct/incorrect)
- Evidence Recall (specific facts or data mentioned by ChatGPT)

Independent Variables (Experimental Conditions)
- ChatGPT Stance: Pro, Anti, Neutral
- ChatGPT Tone: Friendly, Neutral, Confrontational
- Topic Category: Politics, Religion, LGBT Rights, Social Issues
- Pre-loaded Data/Facts (content given to ChatGPT)
- Conversation Length (in minutes)

Control Variables
- Participant Demographics: Age, Gender, Political Leaning
- Prior Familiarity with Topic
- Time of Day (fatigue effects)
- Pre-Session Mood (baseline emotional state)
- AI Interaction Experience
- Debate/Discussion Experience
- Recording Quality (lighting, audio clarity)

Derived/Composite Metrics (For Analysis)
- Constructive Listening Index (combined score):
  * Summary accuracy (40%)
  * Emotion stability (20%)
  * Vocal tone consistency (20%)
  * Validation words per minute (20%)
- Empathy Score (facial + vocal + linguistic features)
- Emotional Regulation Index (volatility inverse)
- Engagement Level (combination of attention markers)
- Defensiveness Score (confrontational language + negative emotions)
- Openness Score (questions + validation + perspective-taking)
